RESTORED.

I was born on a cold rainy morning right in my father’s sitting room. Needless to say nobody was expecting me that early especially my mom who at that time had been deep into her study of Greek mythology. According to her, everything had happened in a blur. She had yelled for my dad who had yelled back, after a couple of yells,they had managed the delivery between them before my dad could get her to the hospital. Right there, on that blood soaked floor, she had some sort of epiphany and decided to name me, ATHENA.
Twenty one years later and I had to explain at least twice daily why a dark skinned girl from Enugu state was named after an ancient Greek goddess.

“Hello.” said the young lady easing herself into the seat beside me. “Hi.” I answered nonchalantly, not bothering to put down my worn out copy of “Tess of D’ubervilles.”Maybe she would go away. “Tess of D’uberviles, huh? I found it intriguing.”
“Yeah.” I said, lowering my head down to continue reading. I wasn’t much for conversations and I didn’t trust girls yet. Before long, I was so wrapped up in Tess’ mess that I forgot entirely that she was still there.
“Athena.”
My enlarged eyes probably gave my surprise away. Well it was surprising that she knew my name. Nobody knew my name. “Athena, I would really love to speak with you please.” Rolling my eyes slightly, I put my book down and met her gaze fully for the first time. She had pretty eyes and her nose seemed to a little bent, but other than that, her face was quite generic. “How can I help you?”
“I know that this might be the last thing you want to hear today, particularly from a stranger but I just need you to know that Mmayen has forgiven you. She wants you to know that she holds no grudges.”
The blood drained from my face as everywhere suddenly lost focus. After many years of convincing myself that I didn’t care if Mmayen ever forgave me. But could it be? It had been so many years ago and I hadn’t seen Mmayen since my mom had come to take me after my uncle died. One look at me and my mother had known that I had something to do with it. And he has forgiven all.” Even though it seemed as though I had left her behind, I felt something unraveling, something I was very sure I wouldn’t be able to stop.

Lawal smiled as he heard Athena and her mother arguing in the parlour. The past few weeks had been refreshing. Athena no longer walked around like she was carrying the weight of the world on her shoulders. Jolene on the other hand was smiling more. Yesterday she had put on make-up. She had also confided in him that she had not touched a single bottle since the day they talked. Athena was always asking him questions about God and had started attending a Methodist church nearby.
“Lawal!”
“Yes ma!Coming ma…”
“Sit down with us a bit, the plates can wait. Please.” Smiling, he sat across from Athena.
He thought about how the past few weeks had turned out, the joy that filled his heart was overwhelming. Athena was smiling at him now, she looked away when their eyes met. Was she shy? He found himself thinking of her more often than normal these days, Maybe. He collected himself. Nobody could heal and bring together like Abba could. Nobody could ease the pain and loneliness of leaving his family like Abba. Maybe one day, he would stand restored like this family, just maybe. He smiled even now as he felt assurance well up within him.
